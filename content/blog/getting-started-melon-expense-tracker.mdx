## Why Melon Exists

Managing money as a couple should not require a spreadsheet, a calculator, and a degree in accounting. But somehow, that is what it feels like for a lot of people. You split the grocery bill, your partner covers dinner, you pay for the streaming service, they handle the utilities, and by the end of the month, nobody knows who owes what or where the money actually went.

Melon was built to fix that. It is a shared expense tracker designed specifically for couples who want to stay on top of their finances together without the friction that money management usually creates. No complicated setup. No financial jargon. Just a clean, simple way to track what you spend, split costs fairly, and keep your money conversations productive.

This guide walks you through everything you need to get started.

## Step 1: Sign Up for Your Account

Getting into Melon takes less than a minute. Open the app and create your account using your email address. You will receive a confirmation to verify your email, and then you are in.

A few things to know about your account:

- Your personal data is private and encrypted
- You control what is shared with your partner and what stays personal
- You can use Melon on your phone, tablet, or computer, and everything stays in sync

Once your account is set up, you will land on your dashboard. It will look empty right now, but that changes quickly.

## Step 2: Create Your Household

Your household is the shared space where you and your partner track expenses together. Think of it as a shared financial home base that both of you can access.

To create your household:

1. Tap the option to create a new household from your dashboard
2. Give it a name (your last names, a fun nickname, whatever you like)
3. Choose your preferred currency
4. Set your household's default split ratio (50/50 is the default, but you can change this anytime)

The split ratio is important. If you and your partner earn similar amounts, a 50/50 split usually feels fair. If there is a significant income difference, you might want to set a proportional split, like 60/40 or 70/30, based on your respective incomes. You can always adjust this later, and you can override it on individual expenses when needed.

## Step 3: Invite Your Partner

A shared expense tracker is only useful when both people are using it. Once your household is created, invite your partner to join.

You can send an invitation directly from the household settings. Your partner will receive an invite link. They sign up for their own account (if they do not already have one) and accept the invitation to join your household.

Once they are in, you both have full access to the shared dashboard, expenses, and reports. Everything either of you adds is visible to both partners in real time.

### Tips for Getting Your Partner On Board

If your partner is hesitant about tracking expenses, here are a few things that might help:

- **Frame it as a team effort, not surveillance.** Melon is about managing your shared finances better, not monitoring each other's spending.
- **Start small.** You do not need to track every personal purchase. Begin with just your shared expenses and expand from there if you want to.
- **Show, do not tell.** Set up the app yourself, add a few expenses, and then show your partner how easy it is. Most people are more receptive when they can see the tool in action.

## Step 4: Add Your First Expense

Now for the part that makes everything work: logging expenses. Adding an expense in Melon is designed to take about ten seconds.

Here is what you enter:

- **Amount:** How much was it?
- **Category:** What kind of expense was it? (Groceries, dining out, transportation, utilities, entertainment, and so on)
- **Paid by:** Who actually paid?
- **Split:** How should it be divided? (Use your default ratio or customize for this specific expense)
- **Date:** When did it happen?
- **Note (optional):** A quick description if you want one

That is it. Once you save the expense, Melon automatically calculates who owes what based on the split you selected. Over time, it maintains a running balance between you and your partner so you always know where you stand.

### What to Track

You and your partner should decide together what goes into your shared tracker. At minimum, most couples track:

- Rent or mortgage payments
- Utilities (electricity, water, internet, phone)
- Groceries
- Dining out together
- Shared subscriptions (streaming, apps, services)
- Transportation costs
- Household supplies

Some couples also track personal spending to get a complete picture of where all their money goes. Others keep personal spending separate and only use Melon for shared costs. Either approach works. The key is agreeing on the scope together so you are both tracking the same way.

## Step 5: Customize Your Settings

Once you have the basics set up, take a few minutes to customize Melon to fit your lifestyle. Here are the settings worth adjusting.

### Categories

Melon comes with a set of default expense categories, but you can add custom ones that match how you actually spend. If you have a pet together, add a "Pet" category. If you are [saving for a wedding](/blog/saving-for-wedding-as-couple), create a "Wedding" category. If you are in a [long-distance relationship](/blog/managing-finances-long-distance), add a "Travel/Visits" category.

Custom categories make your expense data more useful because you can see exactly where money goes in the areas that matter to you.

### Notifications

Choose how and when you want to be notified. Melon can alert you when your partner adds an expense, when your balance with your partner crosses a certain threshold, or when it is time for your [monthly check-in](/blog/monthly-expense-review-couples).

Set these to a level that keeps you informed without being annoying. Most couples find that a daily summary works better than real-time notifications for every small expense.

### Budget Limits

If you want to set spending limits for specific categories, Melon lets you define monthly budgets. When you approach or exceed a budget, you will both see it reflected in your dashboard. This is especially useful for variable categories like dining out or entertainment where spending can quietly creep up without anyone noticing.

## Making the Most of Melon

### Log Expenses Right Away

The number one habit that makes expense tracking work is logging purchases at the moment they happen. It takes ten seconds when you do it immediately. It takes ten minutes when you try to reconstruct a week's worth of expenses from memory and bank statements.

Keep Melon on your phone's home screen so it is always one tap away. The easier it is to access, the more consistently you will use it.

### Review Your Dashboard Weekly

Your Melon dashboard gives you a snapshot of your shared spending, your balance with your partner, and your progress against any budgets you have set. Glancing at this once a week keeps you roughly aware of where things stand and prevents end-of-month surprises.

A deeper dive once a month during your [financial check-in](/blog/monthly-expense-review-couples) is where the real insights come from. But the weekly glance keeps things from drifting too far between reviews.

### Settle Up Regularly

Melon tracks who owes whom at all times, but that does not mean you should let the balance run indefinitely. Settle up at a regular interval that works for both of you, whether that is weekly, biweekly, or monthly.

Regular settlement prevents the balance from growing to an amount that feels uncomfortable for either person. It also makes the whole system feel more current and active.

### Use the Reports

Over time, your expense data tells a story. Melon's reports show you spending trends by category, how your spending changes month to month, and how your split is working out in practice.

These reports are incredibly valuable during your monthly reviews. They turn abstract feelings about money into concrete data you can discuss and act on. A couple that thinks they spend too much on eating out can open the report, see the actual number, and make an informed decision about what to adjust.

## Troubleshooting Common Early Questions

### What if we forget to log an expense?

Add it later. Melon lets you backdate expenses, so if you remember on Thursday that you forgot to log Tuesday's grocery run, just add it with the correct date. The data is still valuable even if it is a few days late.

### What if we disagree about how to split something?

Talk about it. Every expense has a customizable split, so you can handle one-off situations without changing your overall default. If one partner bought something that primarily benefits them, split it 80/20 or even 100/0 for that specific expense.

### What if one of us stops using the app?

This usually happens when tracking starts to feel like a chore. If your partner loses momentum, revisit why you started tracking together and consider simplifying what you track. Sometimes reducing the scope, like only tracking expenses above $20, is enough to bring someone back on board.
